SOUTH BORNEO

South Kalimantan is one of the provinces in Indonesia located on the island of Borneo. The capital is Banjarmasin. The Province of South Kalimantan has an area of 37,530.52 km² with a population of almost 3.7 million.

The Culture of South Borneo

STRATEGIC

This province has 11 districts and 2 cities. South Kalimantan DPRD with Decree No. 2 of 1989 dated 31 May 1989 stipulated 14 August 1950 as the Anniversary of the Province of South Kalimantan. August 14, 1950 through Government Regulation RIS No. 21 of 1950, was the date of the establishment of the Kalimantan province, after the dissolution of the Republic of Indonesia United States (RIS), with governor Doctor Moerjani. Historically the area of South Kalimantan was originally formed as the residency of South Kalimantan within the Kalimantan Province itself.

CLIMATE

In general the South Kalimantan area consists of two seasons, namely the rainy season and the dry season (heat). The rainy season usually occurs from October to May, when the wind blows from the Northeast, the wind speed of each month ranges from 8-14 knots and the monthly average is between 5-6 knots. While the dry season (heat) occurs from June to August and between the two seasons there is a transition season. The air temperature in one place is determined by the high and low of the place on the sea surface and the distance from the beach. The air temperature data reported by the Meteorology and Geophysics Agency, Syamsuddin Noor Station, the maximum air temperature in the Kalimantan area. The range ranges from 33.1 ° C - 35 ° C, the minimum air temperature ranges from 22.6 ° C - 23.8 ° C. The average temperature ranges from 15.6 ° C to 26.9 ° C. While the average air humidity ranges from 77% -91% every month..

In this siring there are other tourist destinations such as Menara Pandang and floating markets made by the Banjarmasin City Government. Now, the tourist magnet is added to the existence of this statue of a brown-skinned ape and a white nose. The position of this statue is a little far from Menara Pandang and the floating market, but still in one area. It is right opposite Taher Square, near the Dewi Bridge. This 6.5-meter-high statue has just been laid out as a game for residents to relax. Although the nearby siring is still under construction and looks not so neat yet, day and night the area around this statue is always crowded with visitors. The visitors usually take pictures there.
